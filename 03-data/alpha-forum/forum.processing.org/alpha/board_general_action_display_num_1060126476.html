I think I've said this elsewhere in these forums in the past, so here goes: don't let future concerns about a job be your sole reason for going to university; it's a mindset geared towards slavery to the nine to five, and if you follow it, it'll make study a grind, a pain, and you'll end up hating whatever you're studying.  
 
Go because you want to learn what they have to teach, and just make sure that what they have to teach is what you want to learn; some universities these days are just java-programmer factories.
 
Disclaimer: I am saying this from the shores of Australia, where I've not found my own lack of a degree (I attended 2 years of BMech/BCS before withdrawing) to be a barrier of entry to the workforce. The US is likely quite different.
