Hi p,
 
Two months ago, i was exactly in your situation: never heard about serial communication, very small notions in electronics, no more in physics --> the real newbie.
 
I bought the bx-24 (just the chip, not the kit), a breadboard and a 7805 regulator (very easy to find, unlike the bx-24). Plus a small collection of sensors...
 
By following the instructions on tom Igoe's url, the examples given on the bx-24 website, and the bx-24 related yahoo group (http://groups.yahoo.com/group/basicx/), it took me just one day to plug different sensors (temp, pressure, light and potentiometers) and get values in p5. 3 days later, i tried controlling a servo motor, and... it worked
 
Now, i'm working on a more advanced project (webcam color tracking + servos). I'll post it in a few days...
 
So don't hesitate: you'll get fast, very gratifying results.
